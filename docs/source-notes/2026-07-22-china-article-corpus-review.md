## Boundary and decision rule

This ledger records one candidate per supplied external article. Two supplied discussion-only prompts have no external canonical article and are intentionally absent. Discovery-platform identities, links, metrics, usernames, and commentary are not stored anywhere in the tracked project.

An HTTP response or visible landing page is an access check, not a source read. A candidate may become `drafted` only after the complete canonical article, metadata, necessary primary records, independent corroboration, later developments, claim ledger, and limitations have been reviewed. A headline, snippet, archive listing, social repost, or redirect is insufficient.

Every candidate is currently `withheld`. This is a deliberate fail-closed result, not a claim that all sources are unusable. The stopping point is recorded per row: blocked sources require authorized or publisher-authorized access; dead or unstable sources require a durable canonical record; reachable sources stop before `source-read` because full-text and supporting-evidence review is incomplete. Consequently, none links to a Dispatch and none can enter public routes, relationships, or the Evidence Atlas.

Access labels describe the 2026-07-22 review attempt:

- `reachable`: the candidate endpoint resolved, but evidence review remains incomplete;
- `paywalled`: full canonical text requires authorized subscriber access;
- `restricted`: the publisher endpoint blocked or did not expose reviewable full text;
- `unstable`: the endpoint or repost is not durable enough to serve as canonical evidence;
- `unavailable`: the supplied article or legacy host could not be retrieved.
