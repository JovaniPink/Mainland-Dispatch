The sourcebook is a research intake artifact, not a public Atlas source. Its
value is the editorial distinction it asks the project to enforce: an
announcement, an API, downloadable weights, a license, a technical report, and
an independently usable deployment are different evidentiary states.

## Editorial decision

Build a third Atlas case named `open-model-release-ledger`. Do not build a
“China is winning open source” scoreboard. The reviewed material does not
provide a stable, independently normalized comparison of capability, cost,
adoption, legal openness, safety, or ecosystem depth.

The public case instead contains three four-step chains:

1. `release-state-not-label` separates announcement, access, artifacts and
   legal terms, and independent verification.
2. `precedents-not-nationality` records a narrow chronology across Meta,
   DeepSeek, and Qwen without treating chronology as national performance.
3. `firms-not-one-actor` keeps official policy language, technical records,
   founder statements, and company promises in separate evidentiary roles.

## Kimi K3 temporal boundary

The July 16 launch page is direct evidence that Kimi K3 was available through
Moonshot products and API and that Moonshot promised full weights and a
technical report by July 27. It is not evidence, at this July 22 snapshot, that
the following existed publicly:

- downloadable full weights;
- a final model card or license;
- inference code or documented runtime support;
- checksums or other artifact-integrity records;
- the promised technical report; or
- independent benchmark, safety, cost, or production-workload evaluation.

The source carries a recheck policy for July 27 or later. That date is not
modeled as a completed event. Any update must directly inspect the released
artifacts and produce a new Atlas version; silently rewriting the old snapshot
would erase what was knowable on July 22.

## Structural non-claims

- `places` is empty. Company nationality does not create a documented route.
- `relations` is empty. Policy language and release timing do not prove
  coordination, causation, ownership, or a supply chain.
- `series` is empty. First-party benchmarks, API prices, parameter counts,
  download totals, and market estimates have incompatible definitions and do
  not form a defensible common chart.
- `relatedDispatchIds` is empty. The review queue remains private even though
  several records informed the same editorial research.
- `dossierSlug` is absent. The case is source-led and does not inherit a dossier
  merely to satisfy a previous Atlas shape.

These empty structures are intentional evidence decisions, not missing data to
be filled with inference.

## Update and correction procedure

1. Reopen the canonical Kimi page on or after July 27 and record retrieval time.
2. Follow direct artifact links and inspect the license, model card, inference
   instructions, checksums, and technical report independently.
3. Promote additional leads only after `source-read` review and exact canonical
   URL matching.
4. Keep vendor benchmark and economics statements attributed unless an
   independent source reproduces them under a declared method.
5. Add independent reporting for adoption or market events only when the report
   itself has been read and its denominator and time window are recorded.
6. Publish a versioned correction or new snapshot rather than backdating later
   knowledge into `2026.07.22-1`.
