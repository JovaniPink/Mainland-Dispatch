## Editorial decision

The supplied brief is useful because it supplies both a current cluster and a
multi-year spine. Its tier labels and explicit stopping point are retained as
research context, but they are not imported as Mainland Dispatch verification.
The app's own `reviewState`, `evidenceStatus`, source notes, and public-status gate
remain authoritative.

The strongest story is not “China chose open and America chose closed.” The
historical record now in the Desk shows several overlapping strategies:

- Meta made a major US open-weight push with Llama 2, Llama 3, and Llama 3.1;
- DeepSeek described open releases as research, recruiting, and reputation
  strategy before R1 became a global market event;
- US export controls targeted chips and, briefly, access to particular hosted
  models;
- Chinese labs combined downloadable artifacts with commercial APIs, custom
  licenses, and undisclosed training inputs;
- self-hosting can matter for privacy and operational control even when it is not
  the lowest-cost serving option.

That record supports a more durable Mainland Dispatch question:

> Which parts of the AI stack are open, who controls the parts that are not, and
> how do those boundaries change under commercial and political pressure?

## Corrections and refinements

### Dates and canonical records

- ChinaTalk's current _China's Mythos Moment_ page is dated **20 July 2026**, not
  16 July as stated in the supplied brief.
- Meta's own Llama 2, Llama 3, Llama 3.1, and Zuckerberg strategy posts replace
  the brief's secondhand Llama history where possible.
- Hugging Face's own incident disclosure is the controlling source for its use of
  GLM 5.2; The Stack remains useful secondary reporting.
- Anthropic's redeployment post is the controlling source for Anthropic's timeline
  and safeguards position, not an independent account of government motives.
- The SCIO/Xinhua page is retained as an official English text of Xi Jinping's
  July 17 address. It establishes policy language, not the motives of Moonshot,
  Alibaba, DeepSeek, or individual model licenses.

### Language discipline

- Use **open weights** unless the accompanying code, data, training process, and
  license justify a broader open-source claim.
- Do not say the chip controls “predicted” or “caused” DeepSeek. Present the 2022
  analysis as a dated hypothesis and investigate the causal steps.
- Do not convert the a16z/Casado anecdote into market share.
- Do not convert an informal report of a deployment, price, model identity, or
  product architecture into a factual claim.
- Do not describe China or the United States as a single AI actor. Firms within
  each jurisdiction have different business models, access constraints, and
  release strategies.
