The external article is the candidate source. Hacker News is a separate

## Structured HN provenance

Each lead records the exact Algolia-resolved `hnStoryId` and the supplied
points/comments snapshot dated 2026-07-22. The Desk links directly to the HN

in the reader or used for sorting.

The Ask HN submission about YouTube moderation is itself a discussion lead. Its
user test and inflammatory examples are not evidence of a platform policy and
must not be reproduced as editorial copy.

## Comment quality rule

HN comments may contribute perspective only after the complete relevant thread
has been reviewed. Inclusion favors comments that provide at least one of:

1. a concrete counterargument with its assumptions exposed;
2. relevant firsthand technical or operational experience, clearly labeled as
   anecdotal;
3. a checkable primary source, dataset, court record, standard, or technical
   artifact;
4. a correction that identifies a specific factual or methodological error;
5. a thoughtful distinction the article collapses, such as announcement versus
   implementation, arrest versus conviction, correlation versus causation, or
   parliamentary motion versus judicial finding;
6. explicit uncertainty, limitations, or a falsifiable question that improves
   the next source-review step.

Exclude comments whose value depends primarily on points, author reputation,
agreement with the editor, jokes, slogans, outrage, national stereotypes,
unsupported geopolitical claims, repetition, personal attacks, slurs, or claims
that votes represent consensus. Deleted or flagged state is not evidence of
censorship without an archive and platform-level context.

Selected themes should normally be paraphrased without usernames or long
quotations. A Dispatch `commentaryReference` links the HN thread and is labeled
`commentary-context`; it never replaces the canonical article or a primary
record supporting a factual claim.

## Critical source-review flags

- The rare-earth story reports a plan; it does not establish an enacted export
  ban.
- The thorium story includes an archive, but the supplied Science and Technology
  Daily URL is truncated. It remains an unresolved corroboration target rather
  than a catalog URL. Experimental uranium production does not itself establish
  commercial fuel-cycle scale or energy independence.
- The Evergrande item concerns US Chapter 15 recognition/protection, not a
  generic finding that the company had completed bankruptcy or liquidation.
- Arrest and allegation stories require their later court record.
- Genocide-related items must distinguish reporting, commissioned legal opinion,
  parliamentary motion, executive determination, and judicial finding.
- Company, advocacy, and platform statements are primary for what their issuer
  said or did, not independent proof of the underlying dispute.
- The Akamai email-view URL is unstable and needs a canonical company notice.
- The Trustnodes bitcoin-executive exit-ban claim is low confidence and cannot be
  used without strong corroboration.
- Modeled avoided-oil and estimated hash-rate claims require their denominator,
  method, geography, and counterfactual.
- Older technical and regulatory guides are historical snapshots, not current
  operational advice.
