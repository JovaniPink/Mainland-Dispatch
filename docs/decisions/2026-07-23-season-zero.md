# Season Zero editorial contract

**Status:** Accepted
**Effective:** July 23, 2026
**Review point:** After the founding Notebook entry has received reader feedback

## Decision

Mainland Dispatch will operate as a **public research notebook** for curious,
intelligent non-specialists who want to understand contemporary China and the
U.S.–China relationship more seriously.

The primary public object is one coherent Notebook entry. Each entry begins with
a consequential conversation, document, or development and follows it into its
sources, assumptions, competing interpretations, historical context and
unresolved questions.

Publication is intentionally irregular. Season Zero may contain **at most three
entries over 90 days**; this is a ceiling, not a promise to publish three.

## Constraints

- Recurring research, verification, writing and editing must fit within
  **8–10 hours per entry**.
- Founding-page product implementation has a separate **6–10 hour one-time
  budget**. QA and deployment have a separate **1–2 hour budget**.
- No additional article-intake batches will be added during Season Zero.
- No automated transcript ingestion, local-model extraction pipeline,
  generalized CMS, new top-level product area or new evidence-status category
  will be built during Season Zero.
- The existing Dispatch, Compare, Trace, Dossier, Atlas, Saved and Desk routes
  remain available as supporting capabilities. They do not compete with the
  Notebook as seven co-equal products.
- The Desk remains private. Evidence Atlas remains an experimental source lab.
- The existing `/archive` route may evolve into a more useful interactive
  evidence surface. It may visualize only published records plus an audited
  aggregate corpus snapshot; it may not expose withheld, rejected or
  source-review leads as public recommendations.

The existing 461-lead catalog is sufficient. Its backlog is not a publication
obligation and is not a measure of reader value.

## Connected public model

The Notebook is where the editor explains what he is learning. The Source
Archive is where readers explore reviewed evidence behind that work. The
private source-lead catalog is editorial memory feeding both.

Interactive views follow the question. Chronology and claim relationships are
the default Archive lenses; geography is used only when location materially
helps explain the evidence. No visualization may turn an inferred relationship
into a sourced fact.

## Publication definition

An entry may publish only when:

1. Every public factual claim has an attributable source.
2. A speaker’s interpretation is not rewritten as an established fact.
3. Mainland Dispatch’s synthesis is visibly separate from the originating
   source’s argument.
4. Forecasts, scenarios, official positions, observations and contested claims
   remain distinct.
5. Institutional context is relevant, sourced and written without insinuation.
6. Source access and transcript limitations are visible.
7. The complete repository quality gate passes.
8. Desktop and 390-pixel mobile production views are inspected.
9. The production site matches the reviewed repository state.
10. No fictionalized material appears in the primary reader path.

## Season Zero test

The founding entry is **“What Xi Jinping Wants,”** following Kevin Rudd’s
argument on *The Ezra Klein Show* into primary records and serious
complications.

It earns a second entry only if representative readers can:

- reconstruct Rudd’s central argument;
- distinguish established, contested and speculative claims;
- explain why 2027, 2028 and 2049 are different kinds of dates;
- identify at least one useful supporting source or open question; and
- say they would voluntarily read another entry in this form.

The editor will also record actual recurring editorial hours and whether the
work remains intrinsically worth repeating.

Possible decisions after the test are **continue**, **shrink**, **reframe**, or
**stop**. Notebook Two does not begin automatically.
